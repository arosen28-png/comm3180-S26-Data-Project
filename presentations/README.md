#### Overview
  1. Research question and hypothesis
     - Question: How much influence do universities have on local economies and urban life?
     - Hypothesis is that we will see:
       1. Rent inflation close to schools 
         - Seeing higher rent prices near campuses
       2. More businesses  that cater to students
         - Higher density of said student-centered businesses around campuses
       3. Sesonal population changes
         - Discovering activity and population shifts during the scholastic year
  2. Data and Dimensions
     - Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System (IPEDS)
     - Yelp or Business Listing Data
     - Rent Price Data
     - Population / Demographic Trends
  3. Planned analyses/initial findings.
     1. Identify University-Centered Areas
        - Begin by selecting a few universities and defining a radius around each campus. This also allows us to compare areas near campuses versus areas farther away
     2. Analyze Rent Patterns
       - Compare average rent prices near universities versus citywide averages or non-campus neighborhoods
     3. Analyze Business Density and Type
       - Examine how many businesses exist near campuses or what types of businesses cluster around schools
     4. Analyze Population Trends
       - Look at whether university neighborhoods show: younger populations, seasonal changes, greater concentration of renters
     5. Compare Across Multiple Schools
       - Compare UPenn, UC Berkeley, U Michigan, and Weslyan University (larger urban university vs smaller colleges/college town schools)
### Final Presentation
#### Overview
1. Research question & hypothesis
        - Question: How much influence does Upenn have on local economies and urban life compared to the rest of Philadelphia?
        - Hypothesis: observable differences between university-adjacent and citywide patterns across crime, rent, and business type
    2. Data
        - Crime:
              * Penn Clery Annual Security Reports (2020, 2022, 2025 ASRs covering 2017–2024)
          * Pennsylvania UCR submissions (Part 1 offenses + FTE denominators, 2017–2024)
          * PPD citywide homicide totals (2007–2025 YTD)
          * UPennAlerts archive (partial 2019–2021)
        - Rent
          * US Census Philadelphia citywide estimates (population density, household size, rent distributions)
          * City Data Philadelphia for University City neighborhood-level estimates
        - Business:
          * Philadelphia Business Licenses dataset (Open Data Philly, 2026)
              - Filtered by University City zip code (19104) and a 1,000m spatial buffer around Penn's campus

    3. Planned analyses / initial findings
        - Crime:
          * Track violent crime trends (robbery, aggravated assault, rape, domestic violence) in the Penn Patrol Zone over 8 years
          * contextualize against citywide homicide data finding
              - Violent crime in the Patrol Zone is roughly flat with no clear secular trend;
              - Robbery trending down
              - Aggravated assault modestly up
              - Citywide homicides peaked at 562 in 2021 and dropped ~60% to ~222 in 2025
        - Rent:
          * Compare rent distributions between University City and Philadelphia citywide using proportions (not raw counts) aligned to the same category bins
              - University City's largest rent tier is $1,000–$1,249 (33% of units);
              - Philadelphia's citywide concentration skews higher at $1,500–$1,999 (26%)
                * Suggests University City rents cluster in a mid-range band rather than at the top
        - Business:
        - Spatial comparison of business composition within 1,000m of Penn vs. outside University City, with license types put into broader categories 
          * Infrastructure (building maintenance, waste systems) is the largest category near Penn at 35%
          * Housing dominates outside University City at 75%
              - suggests campus proximity shifts the local economic profile away from residential toward service/operational businesses
