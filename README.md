# How Does the Presence of a University Shape its City?
---
#### A Case Study of UPenn by Kenadi Crawford, Maggie Goldman, and Alexander Rosen
---
### Main Goal
This project explores how the University of Pennsylvania shapes its district, University City, in comparison to the rest of Philadelphia. Throughout this analysis, we examine whether the areas which surround Penn differ in housing costs, business acitivity, and crime patterns. To accomplish this, rent data, local business data, and crime statistics will be used to determine how the presence of a major university influences Ucity's economic and social structure. Overall, our goal is to understand the magnitude at which universities actively shape local business ecosystems, housing, and neighborhood identity.
### Research Questions
* How do rent prices in University City compare to the rest of Philadelphia?
* What types of businesses are most concentrated around the University of Pennsylvania?
* How do crime patterns in University City differ from other Philadelphia neighborhoods?
* To what extent does the University of Pennsylvania shape the economic and social environment of University City?

### Datasets
* U.S Census Philadelphia and University City Rent Data
  5 years of Data collected on Philadelphia rent prices and Univercity City local level data organized by cluster. Used to conduct a comparison between broader, citywide metrics and district reports.

### How Can This Be Applied Elsewhere?
Beyond analyzing Penn and University City specifically, we hope this project can serve as a guidline for understanding how universities shape the cities around them. By combining rent, business, and crime data, our approach could be applied to other schools and urban campuses to examine how those institutions influence local conditions. Ultimately, this project aims to show how universities coexist with urban environments.
